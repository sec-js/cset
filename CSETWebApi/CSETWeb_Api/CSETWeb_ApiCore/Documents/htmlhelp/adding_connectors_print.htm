Adding Connectors

 
Connectors are used to link symbols and shapes within the diagram and represent the flow of information between components within a system. Connectors may also be referred to as links or lines.
 

Connector

  • Users can link objects using the following connection types:
  • Anchored Connections
  • With anchored connections, the connector remains attached to the same connection point even as the shape moves.
  • Hover over one of the connection points (blue Xs) on the symbol to start a connection.
  • Click and drag the connector to one of the connection points on the desired symbol to end the connection.
  • Floating Connections
  •  With floating connections, the connector adjusts its connection point as the shape moves.
  • Hover over one of the connection points on the symbol to start a connection.
  • Click and drag the connector to the center of the desired symbol to end the connection.
  • This enables a floating connection point for the second object. To enable a floating connection point for the first object, select the connector and drag the connection point to the center of the first object.
  • To change a connection, click the connection point and drag it from the original symbol to the desired symbol.
  • Users can adjust the shape of connectors using waypoints.
  • Waypoints are useful in complex diagrams, in which connectors between different symbols may need to avoid any obstructing symbols in its path.
  • Waypoint are transparent blue dots located on a connector.
  • Select a waypoint and pull the connector in the desired direction.
  • Additional waypoints will appear on the connector as the user adjusts the connector's shape to further manipulate the path.
  • Users can select from a variety of waypoint styles from the waypoint dropdown menu in the diagram tool bar.
