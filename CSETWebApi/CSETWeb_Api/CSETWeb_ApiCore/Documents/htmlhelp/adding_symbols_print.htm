Adding Symbols

 
Symbols are the basis of the network diagram and represent the components within a system.
 

Symbol

  • Add a symbol by selecting one from the Symbols Panel and dragging it to the drawing area.
  • To change the symbol's location, click within the symbol and drag it to the desired location or use the computer's arrow keys.
  • When the symbol is selected, click and drag a blue circle to adjust the size.
  • For shapes, clicking and dragging a blue circle will compress or elongate the shape in that direction.
  • To adjust the shape's size without altering its aspect ratio, hold the shift key when clicking and dragging a blue circle.
  • Click and drag the curved arrow above the symbol to rotate the object.
  • Upon dragging, a tooltip will display the current degrees of rotation.
  • When hovering over a symbol, select one of the blue arrows to connect to a new symbol.
  • Upon clicking, a symbols panel displays and users can choose to copy the original symbol or select a new symbol.
  • Once an option is selected, the original symbol will automatically connect to the new symbol.
  • To replace a symbol, drag and drop a new symbol over the original symbol.
  • While the symbol is selected, use the Format Panel to adjust the symbol's style, text, arrangement, and properties.
  • Note: Some devices may be physically contained within a single shell such as a firewall and intrusion detection system (IDS). In such a case, a logical combination of the firewall and the IDS icons should be placed on the diagram. Or, the Multiple Services Component may be used to group the device icons.
